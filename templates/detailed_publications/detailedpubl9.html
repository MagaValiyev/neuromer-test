Miraculous Reattachment: Surgeons
Successfully Restore Young Boy's Head
After Internal Decapitation Accident

August 10, 2023

In a medical triumph that has captured the world's attention on 14 July 2023 , a team of skilled surgeons at the Hadassah Medical Centre in Israel achieved the extraordinary feat of reattaching the head of a 12-year-old boy who suffered from an "internal decapitation" caused by a serious accident. The incident, which took place in the West Bank, involved the boy, Suleiman Hassan, being hit by a car while riding his bicycle, resulting in a bilateral atlanto occipital joint dislocation, a life-threatening condition where the skull detaches from the top vertebrae of the spine.

The medical team at Hadassah Medical Centre swiftly moved into action, airlifting young Hassan to the hospital, where he was immediately taken to the trauma unit for a complex and lengthy emergency surgery. Dr. Ohad Einav, an orthopaedic specialist who had returned to Israel after a fellowship at trauma centers in Toronto, led the pioneering operation, which lasted several hours.

The surgeons utilized cutting-edge technology and "new plates and fixations" in the damaged area during the procedure, which took place the previous month and was announced to the public in July. Dr. Einav's prior experience with similar surgeries on adult patients during his training in Toronto equipped him to operate on young Hassan, raising the boy's chances of survival significantly.

The boy's recovery journey was nothing short of miraculous, as he defied the odds and showed no neurological deficits or sensory or motor dysfunction. The fact that he can now walk unaided after such a critical process is truly remarkable, leaving the surgical team amazed by his progress. This case was particularly challenging given that bilateral atlanto occipital joint dislocation is rare, even more so in children.

Hassan's father remained by his side throughout the entire recovery process, expressing profound gratitude to the medical staff for saving his only son's life. The quick decision-making, advanced technology, and professionalism exhibited by the trauma and orthopedics team played a pivotal role in ensuring Hassan's survival and eventual recovery.

The successful reattachment surgery has not only brought hope and joy to Hassan's family but also highlighted the remarkable advancements in medical science and the importance of unwavering dedication from healthcare professionals. The achievement has sparked discussions within the medical community, offering insights into managing rare injuries like internal decapitation.

The head reattachment surgery marks a significant milestone for Hadassah Medical Centre and serves as a testament to the extraordinary skills and compassion of the surgical team. As the boy continues his recovery with a cervical splint, the medical staff will closely monitor his progress and provide ongoing support.

The inspiring tale of young Hassan's survival will undoubtedly resonate with medical professionals worldwide and reinforce the importance of pushing the boundaries of medical knowledge and technology to save lives. This miraculous achievement stands as a beacon of hope, reminding us all that even in the face of seemingly insurmountable challenges, human ingenuity and perseverance can triumph against all odds.
